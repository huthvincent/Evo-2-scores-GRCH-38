What am I looking at?

Evo2 is a DNA language model. For each variant we feed the reference and alternate sequence to the model and record delta = ALT log-likelihood − REF log-likelihood.

A more negative delta means the model finds the alternate allele more surprising — i.e. more likely to be disruptive. A delta near zero means the change looks tolerated. Delta is a relative model score, not a calibrated probability or a pathogenicity call.

The scores: two models × three strand strategies

Every variant is scored by both Evo2 model sizes, each under three ways of handling DNA's two strands. The table therefore carries the full set of delta scores side by side (each also has its raw ALT and REF log-likelihood):

Strand strategyEvo2-7B (delta)Evo2-40B (delta)
noRCevo7b_noRC_delta_scoreevo40b_noRC_delta_score
avgRCevo7b_avgRC_delta_scoreevo40b_avgRC_delta_score
weightRCevo7b_weightRC_delta_scoreevo40b_weightRC_delta_score
  • 7B / 40B — the two Evo2 model sizes (7 vs 40 billion parameters). 40B is the larger model and generally the stronger signal; the default ranking uses |Evo2-40B noRC delta|.
  • RC = reverse complement. DNA is double-stranded, so the same variant can be read on the forward strand or on its complementary (reverse-complement) strand. Scoring both strands and combining gives a more stable estimate.
  • noRC — forward strand only (no reverse-complement).
  • avgRC — the average of the forward and reverse-complement scores.
  • weightRC — a length-weighted combination of the forward and reverse-complement scores.
