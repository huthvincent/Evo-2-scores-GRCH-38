What am I looking at?

Evo2 is a DNA language model. For each variant we feed the reference and alternate sequence to the model and record delta = ALT log-likelihood − REF log-likelihood.

A more negative delta means the model finds the alternate allele more surprising — i.e. more likely to be disruptive. A delta near zero means the change looks tolerated. Delta is a relative model score, not a calibrated probability or a pathogenicity call. Each variant carries scores from two models (Evo2-7B and Evo2-40B); the 40B model is generally the stronger signal, and we rank by |delta|.
